The '''ornate rejuvenation pool''' can be built in the [[pool space]] of the [[superior garden]] in a [[player-owned house]]. It requires 90 [[Construction]] to upgrade the [[fancy rejuvenation pool]] and gives 3,107 [[experience]] when built. The player must have a [[hammer]] and a [[saw]] to build it. Using a +5 [[Temporary skill boost|boost]] from a [[spicy stew]] and the +3 boost from the [[crystal saw]], one can build the ornate rejuvenation pool at 82 Construction.

Players can restore their [[hitpoints]], [[Special attacks|special attack]], [[surge potion]] cooldown, [[Energy|run energy]], [[prayer points]], and any reduced [[stats]], as well as cure [[disease]], [[venom]], [[poison]], and [[bleed]]. The pool will not remove the [[Corruption (status)|corruption]] status effect. This cannot be done if the player was recently in [[Player killing|PvP]] combat; attempting to do so produces the message, ''"You can't drink from the pool during combat."''

For [[Ironman Mode|ironmen]], level 87 [[Herblore]] (boostable) and completion of [[Regicide]] is also required up to the point of reaching the [[Tyras Camp]], as the only source of [[anti-venom]] is to create it through Herblore using [[Zulrah's scales]].

It is extremely popular among players, as it functions similarly to the [[Pool of Refreshment|pools of refreshment]] (with [[Ring of dueling|rings of dueling]]) and the [[Elidinis Statuette]] (with the [[desert amulet 4]]) in the player's own house and requires a much lower time investment than the desert amulet 4.

The [[goblin decorations]] can be used on the pool to make a frozen ornate rejuvenation pool; note the decorations must be removed before upgrading the pools. A [[Trailblazer reloaded rejuvenation pool scroll]] can also be used on the pool to give it a fiery appearance.
